# The story behind this project

Explanation pages capture the *why* — the project's intent, the rationale behind non-obvious choices, the trade-offs a new contributor should understand before changing core behavior. This is a seed page; the `/setup` phase drafts a first-pass narrative from the repo's `README.md` and any pitch or brief docs, and a human refines it before the first `/plan`.

## Intent

_What this project is, who it's for, what problem it solves. One to three paragraphs in user-facing language — not implementation-flavored. Populated by `/setup` and refined by a human._

## How the pieces fit

_A short narrative (prose, or a diagram plus prose) of how the major components relate. Keep this page at the "map, not territory" level — link to [First reference](First-Reference) for the detail-level breakdown._

## Non-obvious decisions

_For each architectural choice a reader is likely to ask "why?" about, one paragraph. If a decision has its own ADR under `decisions/`, link to the ADR instead of restating it — this section is an index of rationale, not a replacement for ADRs._

## Trade-offs

_What this project deliberately doesn't do, and why. Often the most useful section for a new contributor — it tells them what not to build._
